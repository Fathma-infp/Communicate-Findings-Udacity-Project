Part II - Features and Characteristics of High Borrowing States¶

by Fathma Yusuf Hussein¶

Investigation Overview¶

The main interest was in finding out the main features that are common in high borrowing states of loan listings. Specifically in terms of: BorrowerState, Occupation, IncomeRanges, MonthlyLoanPayment, Loan Terms, BorrowerRate and TotalProsperLoans.

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset consisted of 113937 loan listings with 81 sets of features which were majorly not used aside from the 7 mentioned features of interest. The data was broken down into subsets of high borrowing states and occupations so as to narrow down findings and have a look at the features more distinctively

Distribution of the top 7 state with the highest borrower listings¶

The top 7 states with high number of borrower listings were found to be: CA, TX, NY, FL, IL, GA, OH

CA(California) State has the highest distribution out of the 7 high borrowing rate states by a large margin. This prompts more analysis on CA borrowers and their features as compared to the other top 7 states. These sates will be the main centres around the investigations that follow.

Distribution of monthly payments of the top 7 borrowing states¶

The distribution is more rightly skewed with the numbers of monthly payments reducing as the values of the payment increases. This meant that the higher the monthly payment was, the lower the number of people making payment.

The distribution also showed that the highest payments made monthly are between 150 to 200 US Dollars by borrowers from the top 7 borrowing states.

Relationship between monthly payments and income range categories of top 7 borrowing states¶

A facet grid of histograms showed that borrowers in the income range category of '25,000-49,999 US Dollars' managed to garner the highest number of monthly payment i.e. above 2500 made, highest of all the other income range categories. The lowest number of monthly payments made on the other hand was '0 US Dollars' and 'Not employed' category of borrowers.

From the plots, it further affirmed that the higher the monthly payment, the lower the number of payments made with all categories maintaining majority of monthly payments at between 0 to 1000 US Dollars i.e below 1000 US Dollars
